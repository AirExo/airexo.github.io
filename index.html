Low-Cost Exoskeletons for Learning Whole-Arm Manipulation in the Wild

Hongjie Fang, Hao-Shu Fang, Yiming Wang, Jieji Ren, Jingjing Chen, Ruo Zhang, Cewu Lu
Shanghai Jiao Tong University
Shanghai Artificial Intelligence Laboratory
Paper arXiv Video Dataset

Abstract

While humans can use parts of their arms other than the hands for manipulations like gathering and supporting, whether robots can effectively learn and perform the same type of operations remains relatively unexplored. As these manipulations require joint-level control to regulate the complete poses of the robots, we develop SuperExo, a low-cost, adaptable, and portable dual-arm exoskeleton, for teleoperation and demonstration collection. As collecting teleoperated data is expensive and time-consuming, we further leverage SuperExo to collect cheap in-the-wild demonstrations at scale. Under our inthe-wild learning framework, we show that with only 3 minutes of the teleoperated demonstrations, augmented by diverse and extensive in-the-wild data collected by SuperExo, robots can learn a policy that is comparable to or even better than one learned from teleoperated demonstrations of more than 20 minutes. Experiments demonstrate that our approach enables the model to learn a more balanced and robust policy across the various stages of the task, enhancing the success rates in long-horizon multi-stage tasks or the presence of disturbances.
